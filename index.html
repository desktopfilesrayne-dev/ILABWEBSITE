The Core Problem & Context

The gap between laboratory theory and real-world execution presents measurable risks to student safety and material conservation. The design of I-LAB addresses these core areas directly:

Safety Gap in Science Education

STEM students frequently manifest insufficient practical training regarding equipment utilities and physical hazards, leading to direct issues of careless handling in chemistry and physics spaces.

Legal & Academic Alignment

Our methodology satisfies requirements of Republic Act No. 10533 (Enhanced Basic Education Act) and DepEd Order No. 48, s. 2006, which strictly mandate safety practices to proactively reduce laboratory incidents.

Real-World Realities

Recent events—such as a chemistry explosion in Makati City in January 2025 injuring six students—highlight that preparation shortfalls carry physical consequences. Virtual pre-orientation serves as an essential preliminary shield.

Evidence of Effectiveness

A rigorous comparative study tracked control and experimental cohorts to determine how much the addition of virtual preparation modules improved overall outcomes:

1. Homogenous Baseline

Pre-test scores confirmed an identical baseline starting point between the control (M = 12.9) and experimental (M = 13.3) groups, proving any future progress was directly attributable to classroom interventions.

2. Diverging Post-Tests

While the control group using text-based modules progressed to Moving Towards Mastery (MTM) (M = 18.3), the cohort augmented by I-LAB reached Closely Approximating Mastery (CAM) (M = 22.40).

3. Amplified Performance Magnitude

While standalone modules are highly effective (d = 2.37), integrating the I-LAB web interface yields an incredibly strong "Very Large Effect Size" (d = 3.10), showcasing superior instructional support.

Theoretical Scaffolding

I-LAB is designed on proven, interdisciplinary education models to optimize long-term procedural recall:

Experiential Learning

By simulating equipment procedures virtually, students experience safe, low-risk interactive repetition before working with hazardous materials.

Constructivist Theory

Utilizes interactive 3D elements and progressive feedback steps to allow students to construct advanced practical knowledge out of basic rules.

Technology-Acceptance

The high scores across layout, relevance, and functionality prove that learners embrace digital preparation tools when they are clearly helpful and easy to navigate.
